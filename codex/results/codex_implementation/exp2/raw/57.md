## Response

To solve the given inequality problem, we will analyze the expression on the left and the expression on the right.

### Step 1: Understanding the Expression
We have:
$$
\sum_{cyc} \sqrt{\frac{a+b}{b^2+4bc+c^2}} \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{2}{\sqrt{a+2b+4c}}.
$$

The left side involves a cyclic sum over three terms (where \(a, b, c\) are non-negative).

### Step 2: Testing the Inequality with Symmetric Choices
1. **Set \(a = b = c = 1\)**:
   - Left side:
   $$
   \sum_{cyc} \sqrt{\frac{1+1}{1^2 + 4 \cdot 1 \cdot 1 + 1^2}} = \sum_{cyc} \sqrt{\frac{2}{1 + 4 + 1}} = 3 \sqrt{\frac{2}{6}} = 3 \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} = \sqrt{3}.
   $$
   
   - Right side:
   $$
   \frac{2}{\sqrt{1 + 2 \cdot 1 + 4 \cdot 1}} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{7}}.
   $$

   We need to check if \( \sqrt{3} \quad ? \quad \frac{2}{\sqrt{7}} \).

2. **Calculating Numerical Values**:
   - Using approximate square roots:
   $$
   \sqrt{3} \approx 1.732 \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{2}{\sqrt{7}} \approx \frac{2}{2.64575} \approx 0.755.
   $$

### Step 3: Checking the Inequality
From the previous calculations, we have:
1. Left Side: 
   \( \sqrt{3} \approx 1.732 \)
   
2. Right Side:

Thus,
$$
\sqrt{3} > \frac{2}{\sqrt{7}}.
