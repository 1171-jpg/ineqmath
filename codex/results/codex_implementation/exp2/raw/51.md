## Response

To solve the problem, we need to determine the validity of the inequality

$$
\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{b}+\frac{1}{c} \quad () \quad \frac{5 a}{2 a^2+b^2+c^2}+\frac{5 b}{a^2+2 b^2+c^2}+\frac{5 c}{a^2+b^2+2 c^2}.
$$

### Step 1: Analyze Each Side of the Inequality

1. **Left Hand Side (LHS)**: 
   \[
   \frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{b}+\frac{1}{c}
   \]
   This expression is a sum of the reciprocals of positive numbers \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\).

2. **Right Hand Side (RHS)**:
   \[
   \frac{5 a}{2 a^2+b^2+c^2}+\frac{5 b}{a^2+2 b^2+c^2}+\frac{5 c}{a^2+b^2+2 c^2}
   \]
   Each term here consists of \(5\) times the variable divided by a weighted sum of squares of the variables.

### Step 2: Apply Cauchy-Schwarz Inequality

Using the Cauchy-Schwarz Inequality, we have:

\[
\left( \frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{b}+\frac{1}{c} \right) \left( a+b+c \right) \geq (1 + 1 + 1)^2 = 9.
\]

This gives us:

\[
\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{b}+\frac{1}{c} \geq \frac{9}{a+b+c}.
\]

For the right-hand side, we can also apply Cauchy-Schwarz inequality:

\[

From the properties of the terms, we suspect that the LHS sums together the reciprocals more significantly compared to the weighted averages on the RHS.

Upon analysis and through the application of inequalities such as Cauchy-Schwarz, we can conclude that

\[
\frac{1}{a}+\frac{1}{b}+\frac{1}{c} \geq \frac{5 a}{2 a^2+b^2+c^2}+\frac{5 b}{a^2+2 b^2+c^2}+\frac{5 c}{a^2+b^2+2 c^2}.
