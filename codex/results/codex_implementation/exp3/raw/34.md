The left side is given as:

\[
\frac{a}{b} + \frac{b}{c} + \frac{c}{a}

Using the **AM-GM inequality**:

\[
\frac{a}{b} + \frac{b}{c} + \frac{c}{a} \geq 3 \sqrt[3]{\frac{a}{b} \cdot \frac{b}{c} \cdot \frac{c}{a}} = 3

This suggests that the left side is always greater than or equal to \( 3 \) for any positive \( a, b, c \).

### Step 2: Analyze the Right Side

Now, the right side of the inequality involves two terms:

\[
\frac{C \sqrt{a^2 + b^2 + c^2}}{\sqrt[3]{abc}}
\]

We can express this in a simpler form using the **Cauchy-Schwarz inequality**:

\[
\sqrt{a^2 + b^2 + c^2} \geq \frac{a + b + c}{\sqrt{3}}
\]

Thus:

\[
\frac{C \sqrt{a^2 + b^2 + c^2}}{\sqrt[3]{abc}} \geq \frac{C \frac{(a+b+c)}{\sqrt{3}}}{\sqrt[3]{abc}}
\]

Combining our results, the inequality can be rewritten as:

\[
3 \geq \frac{C \frac{(a+b+c)}{\sqrt{3}}}{\sqrt[3]{abc}}

\[
